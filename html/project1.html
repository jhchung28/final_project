Project 1: Shipping Container Home Inspired by the Moriyama House

The Moriyama House is a residential building located in Tokyo, Japan. It was designed by the Japanese architect Ryue Nishizawa. The building is a collection of ten individual units that are connected by a series of outdoor walkways and courtyards.

Here is the space in-between my adaptations of the Moriyama House.

On the ships, these containers can be stacked up to 9 units high. This image is an iteration of a home design that I modeled.

I made three-dimensional representations of the shipping containers. The width and the height of each container remained the same at 8ft by 8.5ft respectively. The length of the containers were 40ft, 20ft, and 10ft.

The red lines indicate the square area that the home is laid on. This is also an iteration of a possible home design.

The last two images are also possible home designs from a top down angle. Each individual cluster of containers represent a different room and function. The red lines indicate the square area that the home is laid on. This is also an iteration of a possible home design.
